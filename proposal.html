1. Introduction

Everyone loves a good movie!

Movie industry is a multi-billion-dollar business. Lots of movies are being released in every year. All of these movies have different features, budget, cast crew, etc. but one thing is in common - all want to be successful. Success of a movie depends on various factors of past and present. Identifying the right factors can predict the profitability, rating and award winning of a movie that we define as the most important criteria for movie’s success. Some of the factors in predicting movie success are budget, actors, director, IMDB vote count, social fan following and likes, etc. Movie’s success prediction plays an indispensable job in the film industry, some researchers over the years predicted success of a movie by it’s profitability or IMDB rating, In this project, we will test various factors and find a movie’s success model that consider both in addition to award winning of a movie. The success can not be predicted based on one specific factor, what makes it harder to analyze. Our main goal in this project is to find out what are the most significant factors and connections that influence our success indicators by analysis and manipulations of our data, what causes the difference between the ups and downs of the success indicators, and try to explain the reasons for this in a scientific and research way.

2. Data

The data for the project included two datasets that was taken from Kaggle and one dataset that we will scrap from IMDB site later in the project, with the released month for each movie respectively (attached links and code in Appendix section). We joined these three datasets that gathered the most relevant data for our project, discarded unnecessary variables and null values, and manipulated some variables to find helpful new variables for our prediction, as ROI and binary values for Oscar winner and success movies as succeed gathers all three factor : winner, gross and imdb score.

Now, we will summarize some main insights from those charts:

  • There is a correlation between successful movies to successful movies that did not win the Oscar in almost all parameters. as we mentioned above, there is not many observation for winner award movies and that is the reason.

  • All the social impact parameters, such as, movie facebook likes, votes and reviews are much higher in successful movies than all the other groups, even a lot more than successful movies that did not win the Oscar. That is make sense, successful movies have higher social exposure and apparently that Oscar title has a big impact on it.

  • There is not correlation between budget and ROI, which means that production that invested more than others are not have to be more profitability. In addition, the group that invested the most has lower imdb score than others, so higher investment do not insure better score.

As we can see, there is many insights we can find in our data and we will keep investigating these insights and others in the rest of our project.

4. Data analysis plan

The next steps of our project will be an extension to this proposal, we will dive deeper into our new insights and try to find them an explanation by our data and external researches. In addition we will fit to our data logistic regression algorithm that predict and Verifies the main factors for success: profitability, rating and Oscar reward. most of our work is going to be done by manipulation of our existing factors to create new helpful factors to use for the analyzing step. As we saw in the 3th graph, actors and director names are not that helpful in this form so we will calculate their average history imdb score and gross and use these values as parameter, instead of their names. In addition, as we mentioned in the beginning, we will use web scrapping method from imdb site by the movie’s link that we have in the existing data, to find the release month of every movie in our data.

We believe that our vision of success movie parameters is something new, that has not been done yet, and our division of groups, successful in all the three parametrs, only in two, etc, will lead us to many new insights about future movies.

movies$succeed = ifelse(movies$gross < mean_gross,0,
                        ifelse(movies$imdb_score < mean_imdb,0,
                               ifelse(movies$winner != 1,0,1)))

              filter((gross >= mean_gross) & (imdb_score >= mean_imdb) & (winner == 0))

  filter((gross < mean_gross) & (imdb_score >= mean_imdb) & (winner == 1))

two_from_three_no_score = movies %>% 
  filter((gross >= mean_gross) & (imdb_score < mean_imdb) & (winner == 1))

              filter((gross < mean_gross) & (imdb_score < mean_imdb) & (winner == 1))
score_one = movies %>% 
              filter((gross < mean_gross) & (imdb_score >= mean_imdb) & (winner == 0))
gross_one = movies %>% 
              filter((gross >= mean_gross) & (imdb_score < mean_imdb) & (winner == 0))
